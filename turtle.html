Conservation Feature

Velas Beach Turtle Conservation Reserve

Located along the Konkan coast of Maharashtra, Velas has become a model for protecting Olive Ridley turtles through citizen participation, habitat safeguards, and hatchling release monitoring.

Conservation Timeline
  • 2002
    Grassroots Action Begins

    Local residents and conservation volunteers begin organized efforts to protect turtle nesting sites from predators, lights, and human disturbance.

  • 2008
    Structured Nest Monitoring

    Systematic beach patrols and hatchery practices are introduced, improving hatch success and reducing egg loss during vulnerable nesting months.

  • 2012
    Festival with Purpose

    The Velas Turtle Festival grows as an eco-awareness platform, promoting responsible tourism while funding and supporting local conservation work.

  • 2018
    Wider Coastal Awareness

    Educational programs and partnerships inspire nearby villages to adopt nest-safe lighting and beach behavior practices for turtle-friendly coastlines.

  • Today
    Hope in Every Release

    Each hatchling release reflects a successful chain of protection powered by local communities, forest staff, and conservation partners.

Why Velas Matters
Nesting Habitat

Velas is one of India's meaningful nesting stretches for Olive Ridley turtles, and preserving its beaches supports species continuity in the Arabian Sea.

Community Stewardship

Villagers play a central role in protection, proving that conservation can succeed when local livelihoods and wildlife goals are aligned.

Eco Tourism Model

The turtle festival promotes low-impact tourism where visitors learn conservation ethics, helping fund protective action instead of harming habitats.

Future Generations

Awareness programs for students and families build long-term guardianship so coastal ecosystems remain resilient for both people and marine life.
