India's oldest national park, where Project Tiger was born, and where the Bengal Tiger endures.

Conservation Feature

Jim Corbett National Park

Established in 1936 in the Himalayan foothills of Uttarakhand, Corbett was the first national park in Asia and the cradle of India's tiger conservation movement.

Conservation Timeline
  • 1936
    Hailey National Park Founded

    The first national park in Asia, named after the British governor, a landmark for continental wildlife protection.

  • 1957
    Renamed Jim Corbett National Park

    Renamed in honour of the legendary hunter-turned-conservationist who championed the forests he once hunted in.

  • 1973
    Project Tiger Launched Here

    Prime Minister Indira Gandhi chose Corbett to launch Project Tiger which was a turning point for Bengal Tiger conservation worldwide.

  • 2000s
    Wildlife Corridors Established

    Corbett Tiger Reserve expanded to link with adjacent forests, enabling free tiger movement across hundreds of kilometres.

  • 2024
    Population Milestone

    Over 260 tigers, one of the highest densities in the world, a testament to decades of sustained conservation effort.

Why Corbett Matters
Biodiversity

Over 600 bird species, 50 mammals, and 25 reptile species make Corbett one of India's richest wildlife ecosystems.

Tiger Density

Roughly 14 tigers per 100 sq. km of optimal habitat , among the highest densities recorded anywhere on Earth.

River Ecosystem

The Ramganga river sustains mahseer fish, gharials, and over 7,000 elephants in the surrounding forest network.

Protection

Over 700 frontline guards patrol the reserve 24/7, supported by camera-trap networks and drone surveillance.
